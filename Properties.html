Properties of Bell states

  1. Maximum Entanglement: They are pure states, yet their individual qubits (reduced density operators) are maximally mixed (i.e., information is stored entirely in the joint system, not individually).
  2. Measurement Correlations:
    • Measuring one qubit in the -basis yields a 50% chance of \ket{00} or \ket{11}; outcomes are identical.
    • Measuring one qubit yields a 50% chance of \ket{01} or \ket{10}; outcomes are opposite.
  3. Orthonormal Basis: The four states (\ket{\Phi^\pm}, \ket{\Psi^\pm}) are orthogonal to each other and form a complete basis for two qubits.
  4. Invariance (Symmetry): They can be symmetric or antisymmetric. Notably, the singlet state \ket{\Psi^-} is invariant under global rotation (completely unpolarized).
  5. Transformation: Any Bell state can be transformed into another using local operations (e.g., applying Pauli gates to one qubit).
