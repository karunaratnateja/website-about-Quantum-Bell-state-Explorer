Bell states are four specific, maximally entangled quantum states of two qubits, forming an orthonormal basis. They represent the simplest, strongest form of quantum correlation, where measuring one qubit instantaneously determines the state of the other, regardless of distance. They are named after physicist John Stewart Bell.

Bell basis

Four specific two-qubit states with the maximal value of 2 2 {\displaystyle 2{\sqrt {2}}} are designated as "Bell states". They are known as the four maximally entangled two-qubit Bell states and form a maximally entangled basis, known as the Bell basis, of the four-dimensional Hilbert space for two qubits
